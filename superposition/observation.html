SUPERPOSITION:
Observing Realities

Opening reception:
Thursday, November 14th, 2013 - 5.30pm - 8.30pm

ORGANIZED BY Ryan L. Campbell / Madina Stepanchenko / John W. Moody
ADVISER: Giorgio Savona

ARTISTS:
Xuening Bai & James M. Stone / Tom Estes / Mike Calway-Fagen / Darren Jones / Alexey Katalkin / Alexandra Lerman / Emmy Mikelson / Jong Oh / Travis LeRoy Southworth / Magnus Thierfelder


One of the most common functions that humans perform is ‘looking’, but how often do we regard the extent to which this simple physiological gesture defines how we see and respond to the world? This exhibition offers meditations on the concept and practice of observation.

In quantum theory the ‘principle of superposition’ states that a physical system exists in all of its possible outcomes while it is not observed. In physicist Erwin Schrödinger’s famous example, a cat is placed in a sealed box with a vial of poison and a device that will release the poison under certain conditions. While the interior of the box remains unseen, a viewer doesn’t know the state of the cat and so it must be considered both alive and dead. Only upon looking inside does an observer discover if the cat is alive or dead. The observation itself has to be seen as the function that determines the condition of the cat. It is that gesture which collapses all possibilities, into one conclusion, one reality. The ‘act of observation’ determines in which state the world exists.

Technological advancement has expanded our capacity for observation; from astronomers peering billions of years into the cosmic past, to scientists viewing matter on the sub-atomic level. In daily life we Increasingly observe the world through our screens, iPhones and editing apps which allow us to sift through billions of bytes of information, while also enabling us to present ourselves and our environments as we prefer them to be seen. As our modes of observation evolve, new currencies of seeing alter our perceptions and manifestations of reality. Perhaps for scientists, and artists - so often at the vanguard of investigating and recording the changing complexion of humanity - multiple possibilities and the subsequent act of observation, can be considered their initial and primary medium.
